Mercury: The smallest planet and closest to the Sun. It has extreme temperatures and no atmosphere to protect it.

Venus: Covered in thick clouds of acid and hotter than any other planet. Often called Earth’s “sister planet.”

Earth: The only known planet with life, liquid water, and a breathable atmosphere. Our home world.

Mars: Known as the Red Planet due to its iron-rich soil. Scientists believe it once had oceans.

Jupiter: The largest planet in the solar system with a massive storm called the Great Red Spot.

Saturn: Famous for its beautiful rings made of ice and rock. It has over 80 moons.

Uranus: A cold, icy giant that rotates on its side, making its seasons extremely unusual.

Neptune: The farthest planet from the Sun, known for its deep blue color and strong winds.
