Cosmic Knowledge Database

A deep‑space archive for explorers, nerds, and future astronauts.

The Universe

🌌

Observable Universe

Diameter: 93 billion light‑years. Age: 13.8 billion years. Expansion: Faster than light at the edges.

Space expands faster than light, but nothing moves *through* space faster than light.
🧊

Cosmic Microwave Background

The oldest light in existence — a snapshot of the universe 380,000 years after the Big Bang.

Tiny temperature ripples in the CMB became galaxies.
🌀

Black Holes

Gravity so strong that not even light escapes. Formed from collapsed stars or galactic cores.

Time slows near a black hole. Far observers see you freeze.
⚡

Dark Energy

A mysterious force accelerating the expansion of the universe.

It makes up 68% of the universe — and we have no idea what it is.

Stars & Galaxies

🌠

Neutron Stars

Ultra‑dense stellar remnants spinning hundreds of times per second.

A teaspoon of neutron star weighs 4 billion tons.
🌟

Supernovae

Explosive deaths of massive stars that outshine galaxies.

Every atom of gold on Earth was forged in a supernova.
🪐

Exoplanets

Over 5,500 confirmed worlds beyond our solar system.

Some planets rain molten glass sideways at 5,000 mph.
🌌

Milky Way Galaxy

Home to 100–400 billion stars and a supermassive black hole.

The Milky Way is on a collision course with Andromeda.

Quantum Realm

🧪

Quantum Vacuum

Empty space is filled with fluctuating quantum fields.

Particles pop in and out of existence constantly.
⚛️

Wave‑Particle Duality

Light and matter behave as both particles and waves.

Observing a particle changes its behavior.
🔗

Quantum Entanglement

Two particles become linked across any distance.

Einstein called it “spooky action at a distance.”

Space Travel

🚀

Rocket Propulsion

Rockets work by pushing mass backward to move forward.

Space is silent — rockets make no sound in vacuum.
🛰️

Satellites

Machines orbiting Earth for communication, GPS, and science.

The ISS travels 17,500 mph — 5 miles per second.
🌑

Moon Missions

Humans first landed on the Moon in 1969.

Moon dust smells like burnt gunpowder.
